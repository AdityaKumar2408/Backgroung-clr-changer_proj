(: IMPORTANCE OF COLOURS IN HUMAN LIFE :)

It is hard to imagine a life without color. The truth is we don’t all exactly know the colors that someone else sees. We all probably see colors a little differently, different shades, different tones, and hues. We know that color blindness exists and some people cannot see colors and instead live in a world of muted colors. Almost more terrifying to me than color blindness would be living in a world of hyper-color. Where every color appeared neon and fluorescent. Colours form an inseparable part of our life. If we look around and observe, we can see multiplicity of colours which are blending into our lives and they do have an effect on us but this may vary from individual to individual and also on the circumstances. Colours are a part of our everyday life and a life without colours would be boring and dull. Colours have a strong effect on senses especially on sight, touch and taste and also on our emotions and overall personality.
            Effect of colours on emotions, mood, psychology, personality of any individual comes under the domain of colour psychology. Colours affect mood and emotions by influencing the flow and amount of energy throughout the body. Different colours have different effects on human mind and body. Same colour may have different effect on different people. This can be understood by way of studying colours and their effects by categorising them into two:-,
1. WARM COLOURS: like red, orange and yellow which can be exciting, stimulating or even anger igniting.,
2. COOL COLOURS: like green, blue and purple often create feelings of calmness, serenity, content as well as sadness.
Since ages people are using a few colours to paint their homes and workplaces. The colour used on walls of different rooms and even exteriors have a specific effect on your minds and may create flow of positive energy or may be quite depressing. People tend to colour their living rooms in bright colours so that it gives them a gush of energy as and when they enter into it after whole day of work. It rejuvenates them and makes them fresh and energetic. However some people may choose light colours in their living rooms because they want to feel relaxed and calm. Bedrooms are mostly coloured in shades of red, pink or purple to create effect of warmth, cosiness, positivity and love. Kid’s room are mostly painted with bright colours, as kids are full of energy. Moreover choice of colours, even have an effect on the size and lighting of room. Colouring walls with light colours can make a room look bigger and brighter while dark colours can be used in big rooms to make them appear cosy. Areas which are meant to evoke ideas, creativity and passions are mostly coloured in various bright and inviting colours like red, yellow, orange etc. Offices should be painted in colours which tend to increase productivity, efficiency and creativity amongst the staff. Most commonly people use light colours in offices like light blue, very shades of green or even yellow. Even the colour of bed-sheets and furniture used in homes and offices, have different effect on different people.
